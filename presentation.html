Cell Wall

Structure • Composition • Functions

What is a Cell Wall?

The cell wall is a rigid outer layer found outside the plasma membrane in plant cells. It provides shape, protection, and mechanical strength to the cell.

Composition of Cell Wall

The primary component of the plant cell wall is cellulose. It also contains hemicellulose and pectin. In some cells, lignin is present to increase rigidity.

Functions of Cell Wall

The cell wall provides protection against mechanical injury, prevents osmotic bursting, maintains cell shape, and allows transport of substances through plasmodesmata.
