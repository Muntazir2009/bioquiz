Q1. What is the function of the nucleus?
The nucleus stores genetic material (DNA) and controls all cellular activities by regulating gene expression.
The nucleus is surrounded by a double membrane called the nuclear envelope and contains nucleolus, which is involved in rRNA synthesis.
Q2. Why are mitochondria called the powerhouse of the cell?
Because they produce ATP through aerobic respiration, supplying energy required for cellular processes.
Mitochondria have their own DNA and ribosomes, supporting the endosymbiotic theory of their origin.
